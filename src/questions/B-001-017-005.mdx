## The Concept: Peak Envelope Power and Amateur Radio Licensing

**Peak Envelope Power (PEP)** is the average power output of a transmitter during a single radio frequency cycle at the peak of the modulation envelope. For Single Sideband (SSB) communications, it represents the maximum power output during voice transmission.

In the Canadian amateur radio licensing system, power limits are directly tied to the operator's qualification level. The Basic Qualification with Honours represents a higher level of technical proficiency, which comes with expanded operating privileges, including increased power authorization.

### The 40-Meter Band Context

The 7055 kHz frequency falls within the 40-meter band (7.000-7.300 MHz), a popular band for voice communications. Different license classes have specific power limitations on this and other amateur radio bands, designed to balance operator capabilities with spectrum sharing requirements.
