## The Concept: ITU Regions and Global Spectrum Management

The International Telecommunication Union (ITU) divides the world into three primary regions for the purpose of managing radio frequency spectrum. These regions help coordinate international radio communications, prevent interference, and ensure efficient use of limited radio resources.

The three ITU regions are not based solely on political boundaries but on geographical and telecommunications infrastructure considerations. Each region has its own specific frequency allocations, regulatory approaches, and coordination mechanisms. This systematic approach allows countries within a region to work together more effectively in managing radio spectrum.

### Canada's Location in Region 2

Canada is part of ITU Region 2, which includes:
- North America (Canada, United States, Mexico)
- Central America
- South America
- Caribbean nations

This geographical grouping reflects shared telecommunications infrastructure, similar technological development levels, and complementary spectrum management needs.
