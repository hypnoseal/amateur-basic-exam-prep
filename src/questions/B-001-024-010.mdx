## The Concept: Understanding Safety Code 6

Safety Code 6 is a comprehensive Health Canada guideline that establishes safety limits for human exposure to radiofrequency electromagnetic energy. Unlike simple power regulations, it takes a holistic approach to protecting human health from potential electromagnetic radiation risks.

The code isn't just about amateur radio—it covers all environments where humans might be exposed to electromagnetic fields, from broadcast towers to cellular networks, and yes, amateur radio installations. Its primary focus is on preventing biological effects that could occur from excessive electromagnetic radiation exposure.

### Electromagnetic Energy and Human Health

Electromagnetic radiation can interact with human tissue in complex ways. Safety Code 6 addresses this by establishing multiple measurement criteria:
- Induced currents
- Electrical field strength
- Magnetic field strength
- Specific Absorption Rate (SAR)

These measurements help ensure that electromagnetic energy doesn't cause unintended heating or biological disruption in human tissues.
