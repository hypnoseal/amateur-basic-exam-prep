## The Concept: Amateur Radio Band Allocations

The 80-metre band is a critical portion of the high-frequency (HF) spectrum that provides amateur radio operators with unique communication capabilities. Spanning from 3.5 MHz to 4.0 MHz in Canada, this band offers a sweet spot for regional and long-distance communication that bridges local and continental communication ranges.

Band allocations are carefully managed by national regulatory bodies like ISED to ensure efficient and interference-free spectrum use. The 3.5-4.0 MHz range allows for various propagation modes, including ground wave and short-skip communications, making it particularly valuable for local and regional amateur radio operations.

### Propagation Characteristics

The 80-metre band has fascinating propagation properties. During daylight hours, signals typically travel 50-300 kilometres, making it ideal for regional communication. At night, the band can support even longer-distance contacts through ionospheric reflection, sometimes spanning thousands of kilometers with relatively low transmission power.
