---

## The Concept: Amateur Radio Frequency Privileges

Amateur radio in Canada operates on a merit-based licensing system where operating privileges are directly tied to demonstrated technical knowledge and operating skills. The requirement to achieve 80% on the Basic exam or pass an Advanced examination before accessing HF frequencies represents a carefully designed approach to ensuring operator competence.

High-frequency (HF) bands are particularly complex, involving longer-distance communications, more sophisticated propagation characteristics, and greater potential for international interference. These bands require a more advanced skill set compared to local VHF and UHF communications, which is why ISED (Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada) has established specific proficiency thresholds.

### The Significance of Technical Proficiency

By requiring either an 80% Basic exam score or an Advanced qualification, ISED ensures that operators have:
- Comprehensive understanding of radio theory
- Knowledge of international regulations
- Advanced technical communication skills
- Ability to operate responsibly in shared international spectrum

### ✅ Paths to HF Privileges:
