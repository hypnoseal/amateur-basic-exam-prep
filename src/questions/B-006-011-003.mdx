## The Concept: Yagi Antenna Element Design

A Yagi antenna is a directional antenna design that uses multiple elements to focus radio wave energy in a specific direction. The three primary elements are the driven element (where power is applied), the reflector (behind the driven element), and one or more directors (in front of the driven element).

The director elements are precisely sized to create a phased electromagnetic field that concentrates the antenna's radiation pattern in a forward direction. At 21.1 MHz (the 15-meter amateur radio band), the director element length is critically important for achieving optimal performance.

### Wavelength and Element Length Relationship

The relationship between frequency and antenna element length is fundamental to antenna design. For a Yagi antenna, the director is typically slightly shorter than a full half-wavelength. At 21.1 MHz, this translates to an element length of approximately 6.44 metres, which represents about 0.9 times the full wavelength.
