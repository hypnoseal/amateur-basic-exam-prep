## The Concept: Electrical Potential Difference

Potential difference, commonly known as voltage, represents the electrical energy available to move electric charge between two points in a circuit. Think of it like a water tank—voltage is analogous to the pressure that pushes water through pipes, determining how much energy is available to do work.

A **voltmeter** is specifically designed to measure this electrical pressure by connecting its probes across two points. Unlike other measurement tools that measure current, resistance, or power, the voltmeter directly quantifies the potential energy difference that drives electrical movement.

### The Physics Behind Voltage

In electrical systems, voltage represents the energy per unit charge. It tells you how much potential energy is available to move electrons through a circuit. The standard unit of measurement is the **volt (V)**, named after Alessandro Volta, the inventor of the electric battery.

## Real-World Examples

### ✅ Correct Voltage Measurement Scenarios:
- **Battery Checking**: Using a voltmeter across battery terminals to determine remaining charge
- **Circuit Diagnostics**: Measuring voltage drop across components to identify potential issues
- **Power Supply Verification**: Confirming correct voltage output in electronic equipment

### ❌ Incorrect Measurement Approaches:
- **Using an Ammeter for Voltage**: Attempting to measure voltage by measuring current flow
- **Relying on Ohmmeter for Voltage**: Resistance measurement cannot determine electrical potential
- **Interpreting Wattmeter Reading as Voltage**: Power measurement is not the same as voltage measurement
